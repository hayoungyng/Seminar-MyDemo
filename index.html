Repair and Return

-----

Hayoung Yang. 12/12/2024

Audio Essay

This audio essay, Repair and Return, explores the intricate concept of spatial repair by inter-connecting and weaving the insights from John Berger, David Harvey, and Kader Attia. It introduces Berger's reflections on resilience, memory, and destruction, connecting them to the ways spaces embody both injury and recovery. Central to the essay is David Harvey’s dual definition of the term "spatial fix”, as a process of resolving problems, taking care of it through repair, but also as a state of immobility, fixed and unchangeable. These conflicting meanings highlight the paradox of repair: does it restore and liberate, or bind spaces to their past? Drawing on Kader Attia’s perspective, the essay moves on to the concept of repair as a return to the original, not by erasing wounds, but by embracing them as part of the embodied memory. The essay ends up with myself as a speaker, questioning whether hope for return signals the beginning of repair, or if true repair happens only upon reaching the original state.
